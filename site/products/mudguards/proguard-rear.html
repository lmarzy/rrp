ProGuard Rear - Cable Tie

The ProGuard is our amazing new range of FRONT and REAR mudguards for disc brake mountain bikes offering unmatched protection from mud and spray, it’s perfect for Trail, Enduro or Downhill racers and essential for the weekend warrior looking for the very best protection. The ProGuard REAR comes in 2 sizes: MINI and STANDARD and is made from super high impact recyclable polypropylene, the finish is matte black with gloss highlights for the logo’s and centreline.

MINI

The primary function of the MINI is to protect your shock, linkages, and bearings from getting hammered at high velocity from mud and spray off the back wheel, it is NOT designed to protect the rider. There are multiple fitting configurations via snap-off holes and wide holes on the arms that allow for angle adjustment for various seatstay configurations.

EXTENDER

The EXTENDER is designed to be added to the MINI above to extend the front to push the mud and spray down through the lower chainstay yoke, this will further protect your lower linkage bearings from getting hammered at high velocity from mud and spray off the back wheel. Alternatively you can just use the STANDARD which does the same all in one product. The EXTENDER has had the thickness reduced to allow the user to cut and trim with a pair of sharp scissors to better fit their bike design with multiple fitting configurations via push-through holes.

STANDARD

The STANDARD does everything the MINI does but more, the rear of the STD mudguard is 83mm longer than the MINI to offer a little bit more protection to the seatpost area, and the front is 136mm longer to further protects your lower linkage bearings from getting hammered at high velocity from mud and spray off the back wheel (this mudguard is NOT designed to protect the rider). The front of the mudguard has had the thickness reduced to allow the user to cut and trim with a pair of sharp scissors to better fit their bike design. There are multiple fitting configurations via push- through holes and wide holes on the arms that allow for angle adjustment for various seatstay configurations.

STICKER RECESS

The recess down the centre is a nice easy reference to apply our coloured stickers to, but it has a more important job, this recess prevents the sticker having to sit proud on the surface of the guard making it vulnerable to durability and jet wash issues. The supertack adhesive used on our stickers is strong, although it is fair to say that no sticker is able to withstand a sustained attack from a powerful jet wash

Fitting the ProGuard Rear - Cable Tie



Rear mudguard

  1. Slide the guard into position WITH THE WHEEL IN PLACE, you may need to squeeze the sides of the guard as you do so to get it through the sear stays

  2. Hold the guard in position making sure it tracks the tyre (equal gap both ends)

  3. You can now see which holes you will need for the most secure fit to the rear

    Important - If you have a brace between the seatstays - you must attach at least one cable tie to it if you don't have a brace between the seatstays - you must use 3 cable ties on each arm

  4. Remove guard and push out/snap-off any holes needed by putting the fornt of the guard onto the workbench, and using a thin sharp flathead screwdriver to push out the snap-off holes

    Important - The snap-off hole will break off fast so ensure thre is nothing underneath the guard that the screw driver can hit, failure to do so may lead to damage or harm

  5. Then feed cable ties through teh sames holes from the rear to the front (you must use min 5 cable ties to ensure the guard is secure)

  6. Add clear tape to all areas the guard contacts to frame to reduce scratches

  7. Pull up cable ties LIGHTLY to check alignment ensuring the head of the cable tie is at the botom/underneath the swingarm so it doesn't catch your legs whilst pedalling

  8. Once aligned USE PLIERS TO PULL UP VERY TIGHT, cut off excess cable ties

  9. Before use, ensure the guard doesn't hit the frame when bottoming the fork

Important

  • Zip ties must be tight to prevent movement.
  • Must be a minimum of 15mm tyre & 10mm frame clearance on bottoming
  • RECOMENDED: Use clear protective tape on areas that the guard contacts the fork to eliminate any rub marks or scratches
  • DO NOT USE IN STICKY / CLAGGY MUD AS IT MAY BLOCK AND CAUSE INJURY OR DAMAGE
